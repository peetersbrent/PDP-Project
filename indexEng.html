Kobe Pauwels

After gaining more experience in RallyX Nordic in 2020, Pauwels stepped up again to make his full-time car racing debut in the Euro RX RX3 class in 2021 with frontrunner Volland Racing. A trio of podium results were highlighted by a hard-fought first victory in Lohéac, while he kept challenging his more experienced teammate Yury Belevskiy on his way to second position in the overall standings. He then joined the all-electric FIA RX2e championship for the season finale at the Nürburgring, narrowly missing out on a podium finish.

Kobe Pauwels competed in the FIA World Rallycross Championship package after a successful formative career in karting that notably yielded the European Championship crown and seventh place in the World Championship in 2016. Two years later, the Belgian took second prize in the CIK-FIA Karting Academy Trophy before dipping his toe into rallycross in 2019 with sidesteps to the Belgian CrossCar Junior series - winning the title - and the European CrossCar Championship.

CROSS-CART

Kobe Pauwels has been behind the wheel of a cross-cart since he was 12 years old. Yet a special experience because it is not at all comparable with regular rallycross cars. After gaining a year of experience, he made his debut in the senior category. He did this with the team he is still driving for at the moment, Lifelive. This team always provides the right material and the right support for the five times vice-world champion rally, Thierry Neuville and his brother Yannick Neuville. The cross-cart category is something special. Lifelive's experienced mechanics have worked closely with the FIA Offroad Division to proudly present the incomparable "TN5". The team's main focus was to achieve an optimal reconciliation of performance and reliability. But an attractive design also played a major role in the development process.

The cross-cart category is currently emerging in Belgium. This is not the case in Italy, where there is already a lot of cross-cart racing going on. At RXItalia, there are a lot of experienced pilots from whom you can learn a lot but also where there is a lot of competition. Kobe decided to ride this championship for a few years. With good results as a result. Last year he became champion under 18. His Italian teammate Marcello Gallo became champion in the overall standings. A top season for Lifelive you could say. The bar in Italy is very high when it comes to cross-cart racing. Kobe was also Belgian champion in the juniors. At the moment, he's having a flawless season with two first places in Valkenswaard and Buxtehude.

FORD FIESTA

The 2021 RACB Rookie will be at the wheel of the #96 Ford Peerlings there, teaming up with Junior champion Tomas De Backer and Philippe Huart, who is in his fifth season in the Ford Fiesta Sprint Cup this year! From then on, the 17-year old son of ex-rallycross champion Koen Pauwels will contest all races and go for both the overall title and the crown in the Junior Ford Fiesta Sprint Cup! Despite his young age, Kobe, who is still in school, can already look back on a rich track record. Like many other sons-of, he set his sights on karting from an early age, where he became champion in the European Championship, finished seventh in the World Championship and two years later became vice-champion in the CIK-FIA Karting Academy Trophy, before moving on to... rallycross in 2019!

"I want to get a taste of all motorsport disciplines, including circuit racing and as my father is a garage owner and works closely with Ford Peerlings, the conversation quickly turned to the Ford Fiesta Sprint Cup and we let it be known that we wanted to test drive such a Fiesta in Cup version," explains 17-year old Kobe. "That first meeting went very well and when it appeared that a seat was available at Ford Peerlings, we put our heads together. Especially when it came to the coincidence of the different calendars, we had to take a few decisions. For me, the priority goes to the RX3 and Cross Car Senior, but with six participations we can still fight for the title. It is a new discipline for me, and a new challenge, but going flat out in short sprints from start to finish is not entirely unknown to me. In rallycross it takes four or five laps to make a difference, so I am used to giving everything in a short space of time.

AUDI

Kobe started his adventure with the Audi A1 at Volland Racing. A team that has been very successful over the years in the category, Super 1600. The name of the category has been changed to RX3. Rolf Volland is the team boss who keeps everything running smoothly. The top team ensures over and over again that the Audi's arrive at the start of each race in maximum condition. Before Kobe entered the European RX3 Championship, he had the opportunity to test this car a few times. He started with a great result. In the 5 nations brx championship, he scored a debut win and a second place. This on the British circuit Lydden Hill in the Super 1600 category. After the covid pandemic he could, after a long wait, finally make his debut in the European Championship. He did so in the RX3 class at the Spanish circuit of Catalunya.

Kobe has had a great debut season. After the first race in Spain, he was amazed by his speed. He felt good after the race, but he knew there was more to it. This promised for the rest of the season. The second race was an immediate cracker. The magical weekend in Höljes (Sweden). He started in the final on the back row but he managed to turn this around to second place, which is a great result. His first podium RX3 was a fact. In his third race, his childhood dream came true. He won the third round of the RX3 European Championship in Lohéac, France. All weekend long he was in competition with Belevskiy and others. In the end, he was able to bring everything together and take the victory. After a disappointing result in his home race at Spa (Belgium), he was able to end the season with a third place in Montalegre (Portugal). With a second place overall, this was a top season as a rookie.
